About Aruba

Aruba is an easy-going island with beautiful beaches and straightforward transport — good for newer travelers who want a relaxing beach trip with a few adventure options.

Driving: Renting a car is popular and the roads are generally well-maintained. Drive on the right, obey speed limits, and be careful on north coast roads if you venture off the main tourist areas.

Important forms & resources

  • Aruba embarkation/entry card (ED card) — complete before travel when required.
  • Visit Aruba — official tourism site for events, recommended activities and operator listings.
  • Viator — for booking tours and activities.

Top things to do

  • Relax at Eagle Beach and Palm Beach.
  • Visit Arikok National Park for short hikes and scenic viewpoints.
  • Snorkel at Baby Beach and other protected spots.
  • Enjoy Oranjestad for local restaurants, shops, and architecture.

Places to eat (examples)

  • Beachfront grills and seafood spots on Palm Beach.
  • Local eateries in Oranjestad offering Caribbean and international dishes.
  • Try local specialties and roadside stands for authentic bites.

Car rental & driving tips

  • Book a rental in advance during high season for better rates.
  • Check insurance coverage and inspect the vehicle for damage before driving off.
  • Keep to main roads after dark — some rural roads may be unlit.

Sample day / short itinerary

  1. Morning: Swim or snorkel at a calm beach (Baby Beach for beginners).
  2. Afternoon: Rent a car and drive to Arikok National Park for viewpoints.
  3. Evening: Dine in Oranjestad and enjoy a sunset along the shore.
