Practical, beginner-friendly guides to Belize, Aruba, and the Dominican Republic — what to see, what to pack, and how to make the most of your first trip.

Aruba — quick overview

Why go: Aruba is famous for its white-sand beaches, calm turquoise waters, and easy island logistics — ideal for new travelers who want a relaxed beach-first trip.

Best months: April–August (dry season, fewer crowds), though Aruba’s climate is pleasant year-round.

Short tips: English is widely spoken, road signs are clear, and car rental is an easy way to explore beaches and viewpoints.

Top things to do

  • Relax at Eagle Beach and Palm Beach
  • Visit Arikok National Park for short hikes and viewpoints
  • Snorkel at Baby Beach (calm, shallow water)
  • Explore Oranjestad for dining and local culture
  • Sunset sail or glass-bottom-boat snorkel

Sample 3-day itinerary

  1. Day 1 — Arrive, settle in, beach afternoon, sunset cocktails.
  2. Day 2 — Morning snorkel, lunch in Oranjestad, Arikok National Park in the afternoon.
  3. Day 3 — Relax, short island drive to north coast viewpoints, depart.

Practical tips

  • Currency: Aruban florin accepted but US dollars are widely used — carry some small bills.
  • Transport: Renting a car is straightforward; taxis are pricier.
  • Safety: Aruba is generally safe — use normal precautions at tourist sites and watch personal items on crowded beaches.

Packing highlights

Reef-safe sunscreen, light layers, a sunhat, reef shoes for some rocky beaches, snorkel mask (optional), and a small daypack.

Sample budget (per person, midrange)

3 days: $600–$1,200 (depends on accommodation and activities).

Belize — quick overview

Why go: Belize offers world-class snorkeling and diving at the Barrier Reef, easy access to barrier-island cayes, and friendly English-speaking communities — great for first-timers who want marine experiences.

Best months: November–April (dry season) for calm seas and better visibility.

Short tips: Book boat trips in advance during peak season; choose small, local operators who follow reef-friendly practices.

Top things to do

  • Snorkel or dive the Belize Barrier Reef (Hol Chan, Shark Ray Alley).
  • Visit Ambergris Caye or Caye Caulker for relaxed island vibes.
  • Explore inland ruins (Xunantunich) and jungle day trips.
  • Try local seafood and Belizean specialities like fry jacks and ceviche.

Sample 4-day itinerary

  1. Day 1 — Arrive Belize City, transfer to Ambergris Caye or Caye Caulker.
  2. Day 2 — Full-day snorkel/diving trip to Hol Chan & Shark Ray Alley.
  3. Day 3 — Relax on the caye, rent a golf cart or go kayaking.
  4. Day 4 — Optional inland day trip, fly back.

Practical tips

  • Currency: Belize dollar; US dollars widely accepted. Carry small bills for tips and markets.
  • Transport: Domestic flights and water taxis connect cayes — allow extra time for transfers.
  • Health: Use reef-safe sunscreen, drink bottled water if advised, and pack basic motion-sickness remedies for boat rides.

Packing highlights

Snorkel gear (or rent), reef-safe sunscreen, water shoes, insect repellent for jungle trips, rain jacket (short showers possible in shoulder seasons).

Sample budget (per person, midrange)

4 days: $700–$1,500 (depends on diving and transfers).

Dominican Republic — quick overview

Why go: The DR has a wide range — from all-inclusive beach resorts to lively colonial towns and inland adventure — making it easy to match your trip to your comfort level.

Best months: December–April for dry, sunny weather; shoulder seasons can have deals.

Short tips: If you want culture and food, include Santo Domingo or smaller coastal towns rather than staying only in resort zones.

Top things to do

  • Relax on beaches in Punta Cana or Samaná Peninsula (whale watching season in winter).
  • Explore Zona Colonial in Santo Domingo for history and dining.
  • Take day trips to El Limón waterfall or Los Haitises National Park.
  • Sample Dominican cuisine and local coffee.

Sample 5-day itinerary

  1. Day 1 — Arrive and enjoy a beach afternoon.
  2. Day 2 — Day trip to a nearby beach or island (e.g., Catalina).
  3. Day 3 — Travel to Santo Domingo for a cultural half-day.
  4. Day 4 — Adventure day (waterfall hike / zipline).
  5. Day 5 — Relax and depart.

Practical tips

  • Currency: Dominican peso; many tourist spots accept US dollars but keep pesos for local purchases.
  • Transport: Domestic flights or rental cars for more flexible travel; traffic can be slow around cities.
  • Safety: Use common-sense precautions at night and keep belongings secure in busy tourist centers.

Packing highlights

Lightweight clothes, a light rain jacket in shoulder seasons, comfortable walking shoes for towns, beachwear and reef-safe sunscreen.

Sample budget (per person, midrange)

5 days: $600–$1,200 (resort vs local stays will change cost).
