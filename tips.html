Travel tips for newer travelers

Before you go — planning & paperwork

  • Check passport validity: many countries require 6 months of validity beyond your travel dates.
  • Entry requirements: verify visas and entry rules (COVID rules, if any) for Belize, Aruba, and the Dominican Republic on the official tourism or embassy sites.
  • Travel insurance: buy travel insurance that covers medical evacuation and trip cancellation.
  • Make copies of important documents (passport, insurance, reservations) and keep a digital copy in your email or cloud storage.

Packing checklist — essentials

  • Passport + printed copies, travel insurance details, emergency contacts.
  • Lightweight clothing, swimsuit, sun hat, sunglasses, reef-safe sunscreen.
  • Comfortable walking shoes and reef shoes for rocky shorelines.
  • Small first-aid kit, motion-sickness medication, personal prescriptions.
  • Universal adapter, portable battery, phone with offline maps or saved directions.

Money & budgeting

  • Bring a mix of payment methods: a travel-friendly credit card, small cash (USD for Belize/Aruba accepted widely), and a local ATM card.
  • Notify your bank before travel to avoid card blocks, and know the ATM withdrawal fees.
  • Use small bills for tipping and market purchases.

Health & safety

  • Vaccinations: check CDC or local health guidance; routine vaccines and travel-specific recommendations vary by country.
  • Food & water: in Belize and the DR be cautious with tap water outside of major hotels; use bottled water if unsure.
  • Sun protection and hydration: the sun is strong — drink water and reapply sunscreen frequently.
  • Local emergency numbers and embassy contacts: save them before you leave.

Getting around & tours

  • Book reputable, reviewed tour operators for activities like snorkeling, diving, and inland excursions.
  • For island hopping (Belize), leave extra transfer time between ferries and flights; schedules change with weather.
  • Consider arranging airport transfers in advance if arriving late or if you prefer to avoid negotiating on arrival.

Responsible & respectful travel

  • Choose reef-safe sunscreen and follow guidelines to avoid touching coral.
  • Support local businesses and guides — they often provide deeper experiences and help the local economy.
  • Respect local customs and dress codes in towns and religious sites.

Photography & capturing memories

  • Ask permission before photographing people in markets or private settings.
  • Back up your photos daily to a cloud service or a portable drive.

Quick troubleshooting

  • Phone maps offline: download map areas for each destination in Google Maps or Maps.me.
  • If you lose a passport: report to local police, contact your embassy/consulate, and get an emergency travel document if needed.
Starter checklist for a 3–5 day trip:
  1. Book accommodation near your top activity (beach, town or dive shop)
  2. Buy travel insurance and save insurer contact
  3. Pack reef-safe sunscreen, backup phone battery, and comfortable shoes
  4. Download offline maps and copies of booking confirmations
